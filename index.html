My Work

Software Engineering Intern at Catalyze(Jan 2015 - Present)

Catalyze provides HIPAA compliant cloud computing services for healthcare companies and I work as a software engineer where I built a backend and frontend for monitoring and managing infastructure. Catalyze runs on docker and I work closely with our system administrators to develop robust solutios to make their customer servie and maintenance tasks more efficient. I have taken initiative to build various internal systems integrations to provide the tools and data analytics necessary to support the rapid expansion of the sales and marketing teams. Working at an infastructure based company is not only more challenging than an application based company, but significantly more rewarding.

Lead Platform Engineer at Victory Lap(Jan 2015 - Present)

Victory Lap is a mobile social networking platform that connects students with other students who went to their school in their area. I am really excited to release Victory Lap and there have been a lot of challenges along the way. The app is built using Ionic Framework and Parse as a backend. I have worked on optomizing the navigation of the app, developing custom plugins, product development and designing user flows, project management and development of innovative features. Working Victory Lap has been an incredible experience and it is amazing how much a couple of determined computer science students can do outside of the classroom.

Software Engineering Intern at EatStreet(May 2014 - Oct 2014)

Working at Eatstreet I developed marketing and sales analytics tools and infastructure to support various marketing campaigns. I also worked on database management, developed a new way to process coupons and created a dashboard to monitor real-time sales and orders. The atmosphere at EatStreet was fun and relaxing and the culture had a "work hard play hard" mentality. EatStreet was my first software engineering internship and really opened the door for me to find more opportunities.

Emonix Research Project for the University of Wisconsin Madison(Sep 2014 - Aug 2015)

Emonix is a home automation and energy sustainability project. During my time working on Emonix I developed web and mobile dashboards, backend infastructure and demos for competitions. This past spring Emonix won 3rd place in the Wisconsin Energy and Sustainability Challenge. Emonix is a temperature control system that lets the user control which rooms are heated and cooled while the system learns the heating and cooling trends of the user and makes decisions to save energy. Another sustainability project I worked on that stems from Emonix is a water softener monitoring system that is installed in dorms across the UW Madison campus and alerts personnel when it is optimal to add water softener salt. This system has saved more thatn $2,000 in salt per semester per dorm!
